\hypertarget{md_README_autotoc_md3}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Project Description}{Project Description}}\label{md_README_autotoc_md3}
This project explores the concept of a digital transmitter, how it operates, and how signals are generated and prepared for transmission through a communication channel. It delves into the practical aspects of signal modulation and the challenges of transmitting signals through noisy channels.\hypertarget{md_README_autotoc_md4}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Main Activities}{Main Activities}}\label{md_README_autotoc_md4}

\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item {\itshape Designing a digital transmitter\+:}
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Developing a foundational digital transmitter using Python, focusing on modular design and clear signal processing steps.
\end{DoxyItemize}
\item {\itshape Implementation of modulation schemes\+:}
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Amplitude Modulation (AM),
\item Amplitude Shift Keying (ASK),
\item Frequency Modulation (FM),
\item Frequency Shift Keying (FSK),
\item Phase Shift Keying (PSK),
\item Binary Phase Shift Keying (BPSK),
\item Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM),
\item Quadrature Phase Shift Keying (QPSK).
\end{DoxyItemize}
\item {\itshape Simulation of a communication channel with noise\+:} ~\newline

\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Implementing a channel with Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN). ~\newline

\item Analyzing the Signal-\/to-\/\+Noise Ratio (SNR).
\end{DoxyItemize}
\item {\itshape Evaluation of efficiency and robustness\+:}
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Assessing the energy efficiency of each modulation scheme. ~\newline

\item Analyzing noise resistance.
\end{DoxyItemize}

\hypertarget{md_README_autotoc_md8}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Amplitude Modulation (\+AM)}{Amplitude Modulation (AM)}}\label{md_README_autotoc_md8}
{\bfseries{Amplitude Modulation (AM)}} is a modulation technique where the amplitude of the carrier signal is varied in proportion to the instantaneous amplitude of the message signal. It\textquotesingle{}s a simple method but is susceptible to noise.

\hypertarget{md_README_autotoc_md9}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Amplitude Shift Keying (\+ASK)}{Amplitude Shift Keying (ASK)}}\label{md_README_autotoc_md9}
{\bfseries{Amplitude Shift Keying (ASK)}} is a form of amplitude modulation that represents digital data as variations in the amplitude of a carrier wave. In its simplest form, the presence of a carrier wave represents a binary 1, and the absence represents a binary 0.



{\bfseries{Amplitude Shift Keying (ASK) image}}\begin{adjustwidth}{1em}{0em}

\hypertarget{md_README_autotoc_md10}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Frequency Modulation (\+FM)}{Frequency Modulation (FM)}}\label{md_README_autotoc_md10}
{\bfseries{Frequency Modulation (FM)}} is a modulation technique where the frequency of the carrier signal is varied in proportion to the instantaneous amplitude of the message signal. FM is less susceptible to noise than AM.

\hypertarget{md_README_autotoc_md11}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Frequency Shift Keying (\+FSK)}{Frequency Shift Keying (FSK)}}\label{md_README_autotoc_md11}
{\bfseries{Frequency Shift Keying (FSK)}} is a form of frequency modulation that represents digital data as variations in the frequency of a carrier wave. Different frequencies are used to represent different binary values.

\hypertarget{md_README_autotoc_md12}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Phase Shift Keying (\+PSK)}{Phase Shift Keying (PSK)}}\label{md_README_autotoc_md12}
{\bfseries{Phase Shift Keying (PSK)}} is a modulation technique where the phase of the carrier signal is varied to represent digital data. The amplitude and frequency of the carrier signal remain constant.

\hypertarget{md_README_autotoc_md13}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Binary Phase Shift Keying (\+BPSK)}{Binary Phase Shift Keying (BPSK)}}\label{md_README_autotoc_md13}
{\bfseries{Binary Phase Shift Keying (BPSK)}} is a form of phase shift keying where the phase of the carrier signal is varied to represent binary data. It uses two phases to represent binary 0 and 1.

{\bfseries{Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM)}} is a modulation technique that combines both amplitude and phase modulation to transmit more data per symbol. It uses multiple amplitude levels and phase shifts to encode data.
